The idea

One pool. A realm of domains.

The contract is the ERC-20, the owner of a single v4 liquidity position, and the fee router — all at once. To own the token is to own the liquidity.

⬡

Hold a domain

Five mechanics

An engine that rewards conviction.

The "holding is LPing" core, with five systems layered on top — each one verifiable in the source.

1

Loyalty weighting

Fees are split by weight, not headcount. A domain's weight climbs the longer you hold it — up to 2× — and resets the moment you sell. Tenure rules.

2

POL flywheel

A slice of every fee is compounded back into the position. Liquidity deepens over time → less slippage → more volume. The realm grows its own depth, and it's never withdrawable.

3

Dynamic fee

A decaying anti-snipe ramp at launch, a sell premium so dumps pay the holders, and a volatility term — all hard-capped at 10%. Borders defended.

4

Tri-flywheel

Fees split three ways: holders · POL · buyback-and-burn. RLM is burned outright and bought back with ETH, so the supply only ever falls.

5

Govern the realm — with domains that evolve

A supermajority of loyalty-weight can tune the realm's economics (fees, flywheel split) — but only within the same hard caps that bind the deployer, so governance can never overreach. And each domain's on-chain sigil levels up with loyalty: brighter glow, thicker borders, a crown unlocked at the top tier.
